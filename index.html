Hryhorii Skovoroda


The world pursued him, but did not catch

  • 1722

    Hryhorii Skovoroda was born in the village of Chornukhy, Kyiv region, Ukraine.

  • 1731

    His father, a priest, began teaching him at home.

  • 1740

    Skovoroda entered the Kyiv-Mohyla Academy, where he studied philosophy, languages, music, and theology.

  • 1745

    He graduated from the academy and became a teacher there.

  • 1750

    Skovoroda left the academy and began traveling across Ukraine, teaching and preaching his philosophical ideas.

  • 1762

    He published his first book, "A Conversation Between a Philosopher, a Jew, and a Christian," which was controversial due to its criticism of organized religion.

  • 1772

    Skovoroda moved to Saint Petersburg and became a tutor for the wealthy Shuvalov family.

  • 1779

    He returned to Ukraine and settled in the city of Kharkiv, where he continued to teach and write.

  • 1784

    Skovoroda died in Kharkiv at the age of 62.
